## Why LaunchpadX

Apple removed Launchpad in macOS 26 Tahoe. The feature people miss most is
**visual app organization** — dragging one app onto another to make a folder,
laying everything out across pages exactly how you like it. That organization
layer is the whole point of LaunchpadX, and it's built to feel right.

## Features

- 🗂️ **Drag-to-folder** — drop one app onto another to create a folder, just
  like the real thing. Folders open into a smooth glass shelf.
- 🖐️ **Open your way** — a global hotkey, the menu-bar icon, or a four-finger
  trackpad pinch (exactly like the original).
- 🔎 **Fuzzy search (⌘F)** — start typing to filter instantly. Multi-word
  abbreviations work too: `cur pr` finds *Cursor Pro*.
- 📄 **Pages & reordering** — arrange apps across multiple pages; swipe or click
  the page dots to move between them.
- 🎨 **Liquid Glass design** — a blurred wallpaper backdrop (live video
  wallpapers included), tuned to sit right at home on Tahoe.
- 🖥️ **Native & lightweight** — pure AppKit, no Electron, no web wrappers. The
  Dock stays put and the menu bar tucks away while you're in the launcher, just
  like the built-in Launchpad did.
- 🔄 **Automatic updates** — LaunchpadX checks for new versions and updates
  itself in place. Nothing left on disk.

## Requirements

- macOS 26 (Tahoe) or later.

## Install

1. Download the latest **`.dmg`** from the
   [**Releases**](https://github.com/sirpooya/LaunchpadX/releases/latest) page.
2. Open it and **drag LaunchpadX into your Applications folder**.
3. Launch it. Because the app isn't signed with a paid Apple Developer
   certificate yet, macOS may warn you on first open — **right-click the app →
   Open**, then confirm. You only need to do this once.

> After the first launch, LaunchpadX keeps itself up to date automatically —
> you won't need to download again.

## Updating

LaunchpadX has a built-in updater. You can also trigger a check any time from
**the menu-bar icon → Check for Updates…** or in **Settings → About → Update**.
When a new version is available you'll see what's changed before installing.
